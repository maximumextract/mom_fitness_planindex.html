Before You Begin

It Starts in Your Mind

The biggest thing about this journey is learning patience and falling in love with the process. In the early weeks, you won't see results right away — and that's completely normal. You're putting yourself through new, sometimes hard situations where the work doesn't yet show on your body.

The quicker you accept this, the better you'll endure the mental battle. And once you start seeing progress? It becomes addicting. You'll want to keep going to discover your true potential.

This isn't just a diet plan — it's a lifestyle reset. Everything you're about to do, from tracking macros to hitting cardio, is building a new version of you.

Know Your Foods

Mom's Macro Cheat Sheet

Different brands vary wildly. Here are the exact macro targets to look for when buying each food so your tracking stays accurate.

🥛

Greek Yogurt

Per 175g · 100 Cal · 15–19g Protein

Tastes plain on its own but incredible protein-to-calorie ratio. Jazz it up with sugar-free syrup, cinnamon, and stevia. Cinnamon also helps keep insulin stable.

🥩

Lean Ground Beef

Per 100g · 210 Cal · 17–19g P · 12–15g F

Weigh COOKED. Check the package for these macros before buying — they should match closely. Great for batch cooking early in the week.

🥩

Extra Lean Ground Beef

Per 100g · 180 Cal · 18–22g P · 8–10g F

Used later in the cut to reduce fat intake while keeping protein high. Weigh COOKED. Check the package macros match before buying.

🍚

White Rice (Cooked)

Per 100g · 130 Cal · 28.6g Carbs

All white rice is the same — log as "White Rice Cooked" in MyFitnessPal. Make a huge batch every few days. Always weigh cooked, not raw.

🥔

Potato

200g Raw · 154 Cal · 42.8g Carbs

Weigh RAW — it's easier. Air fry or oven bake fresh so you don't deal with leftover portions. If meal prepping in bulk, log as "Potato Cooked."

🍗

Chicken Breast

Any brand · ~165 Cal · 31g P per 100g

All chicken breast is the same regardless of brand. Weigh COOKED. The leanest protein source — your best friend during the deeper phases of the cut.

🍗

Chicken Thighs

Per package macros · Higher Fat

Used early in the plan while calories are higher. Check your package macros. Weigh COOKED. Will be swapped to chicken breast as the cut progresses.

🥚

Eggs & Egg Whites

Eggs: ~70 Cal · 6g P each · Whites: 250g for Meal 4

Whole eggs used early phases. Later in the cut, switched to egg whites only to reduce fat calories while keeping protein high.

🍍

Fruit & Berries

Pineapple 100g · Berries 150g · Banana 150g

Used strategically with meals for natural carbs and micronutrients. Frozen mixed berries work great — affordable, convenient, and just as nutritious as fresh.

The Roadmap

Your Phase-by-Phase Journey

Each phase has a specific purpose. Don't skip ahead — the order matters. Trust the process.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–3

Prime Your Body

2,500 Cal/day
  • 3L water minimum daily
  • Goal: stabilize metabolism, don't shock the body
  • Cardio: 15 min treadmill post-workout (11.5 km/h, 3.1 incline)
  • You may feel bloated or look "worse" — this is normal and temporary
  • Your hormones and metabolism are being calibrated for the cut ahead
  • Chicken Thighs in Meal 3 (higher fat, higher calories)
Phase 2 · Weeks 4–7

Start the Cut

2,300 Cal/day
  • 200 calorie reduction — calories cut from fats, not carbs
  • Switch Chicken Thighs → Chicken Breast in Meal 3
  • Cardio stays at 15 min treadmill post-workout
  • Body starts stripping fat while your muscles look temporarily flatter — stay the course
  • Keep carbs the same to fuel workouts and preserve muscle
Phase 3 · Weeks 8–11

Deepen the Cut

2,200 → 2,100 Cal/day
  • Cardio increases to 30 minutes post-workout
  • 10,000 steps daily target added — track on MyFitnessPal with phone in pocket
  • White rice in Meal 3 reduced from 200g → 150g
  • Egg Whites replace whole eggs in Meal 4
  • Results become visibly clear — this is your momentum phase
  • Never go below 2,100 calories unless training for competition
Phase 4 · Weeks 12+

Peak Results

2,100 Cal/day
  • Cardio jumps to 45 minutes post-workout
  • Step goal increases to 15,000 steps/day
  • You can split cardio throughout the day if 45 consecutive min isn't possible
  • Calories stay stable — only cardio and steps increase from here
  • This is where most visible transformation happens — consistency is everything

Tracking Tips for Moms

📱 MyFitnessPal Rules

  • Scan everything. Use the barcode scanner — it takes 10 seconds and eliminates guessing.
  • Log "White Rice Cooked" — not raw. The calorie count differs significantly.
  • Log "Potato Cooked" if batch-cooking, or "Potato" if weighing raw before cooking.
  • Track steps by keeping your phone in your pocket on the treadmill — it adds to your daily total automatically.
  • If you plan to drink, log it in advance and reduce snacks or carbs that day to stay within your calorie target.

🍽️ Mom's Kitchen Tips

  • Batch cook rice and proteins 2–3 times per week. Keep portions in the fridge for quick assembly.
  • Potato is best made fresh — air fry or bake exactly what you need to avoid waste and miscounting.
  • Greek yogurt needs flavour help. Sugar-free syrup + cinnamon + stevia = game changer. Add berries for texture.
  • Zero-calorie snacks are your friends — sugar-free jello, diet soda, zero-cal sauces to keep meals interesting without blowing calories.
  • TikTok "full day of eating on a cut" videos are great when you're bored of the same meals — just match the calories to your current phase.
